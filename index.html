Wave

by: Josh Pryor

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I was inspired to make this game based on the way the ball in Pong bounces off the walls, I wanted a game that required skill and practice, a game with multiple enemies. I chose this game over any other game because I like the concept of having the player and the CPU to be squares.

2. What I Learned

from user testing, people helped me spot errors in the code and made the game much better. finding and fixing errors and commenting about their preferences on what I can do to make the game better for them. I fixed the ending to make the restart smoother and fixed the collisions and made the game smoother.

3. Architecture of My Game

one entity is the player or user. The player uses WASD or the arrow keys (or both(epik strat)). then there is the enemy. the enemy moves diagonally across the screen. the goal of the game is to avoid the enemy as long as possible.

4. Technical Implementation

one particular aspect of the game that I am proud of is the no corner strat where if you go into the a corner, it starts to deduct the score of the player twice as fast as the pace in which you gain score. Another particular aspect of the game that I am proud of is 'no smooth movement' this makes it so you the player, when you press a key, doesn't move continuously in that direction. This allows for better CPS to be a major factor in your score because you can escape the enemies path much better.

5. Most Challenging Part

the most challenging part I faced was the collision and the math behind how to find when the enemy touched the user. then someone said "why dont U use the collision method" "wat" "the collision method" "oh"

6. Future Fixes and Updates

one improvement is that if the player stays still for over 15 seconds, the user image turns into an apple... to confuse them. another is to make the no corner camp areas bigger so people dont camp on the edge of the corner.
